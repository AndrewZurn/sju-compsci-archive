Lab 17: Manipulating Text

Perhaps you remember trying to speak in "Pig Latin" as a kid. The basic idea is to take the leading consonants (before the first vowel) off the front of a word and add them to the end of the word with an “ay” following (“awesome pig latin” becomes “awesomeway igpay atinlay”). Words starting with a vowel letter stay the same with a “way” appended to their end. If you need to refresh your memory more on Pig Latin, check out this website: http://users.snowcrest.net/donnelly/piglatin.html (MIGHT HAVE A SLIGHTLY DIFFERENT VERSION OF THE GAME).

 

Your goal this time is to convert any string (which can contain multiple words) to its equivalent pig Latin string.  The rules are defined in the description of PigLatin.java file.  The file starts with the basic user interface and includes method actionPerformed. In addition, we have given you a couple of private methods (namely, indexOfFirstVowel and isVowel) which might prove useful for you.

 

Your goal is to complete the convertLine method. You may find it useful to define one or more helper methods in addition to the two that have been given to you.
